The Travelling Salesman Problem

The travelling salesman problem asks the following question: "Given a list of cities and the distances between each pair of cities, what is the shortest possible route that visits each city exactly once and returns to the origin city?"

Here are the distances between the four cities a salesman must go to during his trip.

Here is an example of the Travelling Salesman Problem. If he started in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, which would be the shortest route to hit all cities and return to Pittsburgh?

Due to how the cities are at the four regional corners of the United States, the best path, starting from Pittsburgh, would be to go to Seattle or Miami First, then Los Angeles, and then afterwards, visit the city that has not been visited yet, and then finally, return to Pittsburgh.
